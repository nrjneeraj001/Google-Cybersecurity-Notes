# Module 2 - The Evolution of Cybersecurity

# History of Cybersecurity

## Past Cybersecurity Attacks
- Cybersecurity attacks continuously evolve.
- Modern attacks are often modified versions of old attacks.
- Understanding past attacks helps prevent future attacks.

---

## Computer Virus
- Malicious code that harms systems.
- Disturbs computer operations.
- Can damage software and data.
- Attaches to files and programs.
- Spreads through networks.

---

## Malware
- Malicious software designed to harm devices or networks.
- Virus is a type of malware.

---

## Brain Virus (1986)
- Created by Basit and Amjad Farooq Alvi.
- Spread through floppy disks.
- Infected computers and slowed systems.
- Affected business productivity.

---

## Morris Worm (1988)
- Created by Robert Tappan Morris.
- Spread automatically through networks.
- Installed repeatedly on systems.
- Caused systems to crash.
- Affected 6000+ computers.

---

## CERT (Computer Emergency Response Team)

### Responsibilities
- Detect security incidents
- Respond to attacks
- Recover damaged systems
- Prevent future attacks

---

# Attacks in the Digital Age

## Internet Expansion
- Faster internet increased computer usage.
- Malware spread quickly through the internet.
- Physical disks became less common.
- Attack speed increased rapidly.

---

## Love Letter Attack (2000)
- Created by Onel de Guzman.
- Stole login credentials.

### Working
- Users received an email titled “I Love You”.
- Opening attachment activated malware.
- Malware sent itself to contacts automatically.

### Impact
- Millions of computers infected
- Huge financial damage

---

## Social Engineering
- Manipulating people to collect information.

### Key Points
- Uses human trust and mistakes
- Creates fake trust to steal data

---

## Phishing
- Fake emails or SMS used to steal data.

### Working
- Fake message is sent
- User clicks malicious link
- User logs into fake website
- Data reaches attackers

---

## Modern Risks
- People prioritize convenience.
- More data shared online.
- Vulnerabilities increase.

---

## Security Professional Responsibilities
- Identify risks
- Train employees
- Implement safe practices
- Protect organizations

---

## Equifax Data Breach (2017)

### Impact
- 143 million records stolen
- Millions of users affected

### Data Stolen
- Social Security numbers
- Birth dates
- Addresses
- Credit card details
- Driving license numbers

### Reasons
- Security vulnerabilities not fixed
- Ignored security updates

### Result
- 575 million dollar fine

# Common Attacks and Their Effectiveness

## Phishing
- Tricks users through emails or messages.
- Used to steal sensitive data or install malware.

### Types of Phishing

#### Business Email Compromise (BEC)
- Fake email from trusted person/company
- Asks for money or information

#### Spear Phishing
- Targets specific person or group
- Uses personal information

#### Whaling
- Targets executives or managers

#### Vishing
- Voice call attacks

#### Smishing
- SMS/text message attacks

---

## Malware
- Harmful software that damages systems or steals data.

### Types of Malware

#### Virus
- Needs user action
- Attaches to files
- Can damage data

#### Worm
- Spreads automatically
- Uses networks for spreading

#### Ransomware
- Locks or encrypts data
- Demands money

#### Spyware
- Secretly collects user data
- Tracks user activity

---

# Social Engineering

## Social Media Phishing
- Uses social media information to target users.

---

## Watering Hole Attack
- Attackers infect popular websites.
- Website visitors become victims.

---

## USB Baiting
- Infected USB left for users.
- Device gets infected when connected.

---

## Physical Social Engineering
- Attackers pretend to be employees or visitors.
- Gain physical access.

---

## Social Engineering Principles

### Authority
- People trust someone in power.

### Intimidation
- Creates fear or pressure.

### Social Proof
- People follow others.

### Scarcity
- Limited offers create urgency.

### Familiarity
- Friendly behavior builds trust.

### Trust
- Relationship used for manipulation.

### Urgency
- Forces quick action.

---

# Key Takeaways
- Attacks target both people and systems.
- Phishing and social engineering are common.
- Malware has multiple forms.
- Awareness and training are important.

# The Eight CISSP Security Domains

## Introduction
CISSP domains are major cybersecurity areas used to protect systems, data, and organizations.

---

## Security and Risk Management
- Focuses on policies, laws, and risk management.

### Key Points
- Define security goals
- Reduce risks
- Follow regulations
- Maintain business continuity

---

## Asset Security
- Protects data and physical devices.

### Key Points
- Secure data storage
- Data retention and destruction
- Safe disposal of devices

---

## Security Architecture and Engineering
- Designs secure systems and infrastructure.

### Key Points
- Use security technologies
- Configure secure systems
- Protect data through secure design

---

## Communication and Network Security
- Secures communication systems and networks.

### Key Points
- Secure wired/wireless networks
- Monitor network activity
- Create security policies

---

## Identity and Access Management (IAM)
- Controls user access to systems and data.

### Key Points
- Verify identities
- Assign permissions
- Prevent unauthorized access

---

## Security Assessment and Testing
- Evaluates security systems.

### Key Points
- Conduct testing and audits
- Analyze risks
- Review permissions regularly

---

## Security Operations
- Handles daily security activities.

### Key Points
- Monitor alerts
- Respond to threats
- Follow security procedures

---

## Software Development Security
- Ensures secure software development.

### Key Points
- Use secure coding practices
- Protect user data
- Integrate security into development lifecycle
